Observations, mechanisms, and fragments of thought
On Beginnings
May, Year of Our Work

Every work begins crude and without form. The hand doth hesitate and the line waver. The thought resists capture. Yet through repetition, the uncertain mark becomes deliberate. Thus, one must not wait for clarity, but proceed until clarity emerges.

On Tools
Same Month

A tool is an extension of intent. If poorly made, it interrupts thought. Yet when well made, it vanishes entirely. The highest form be not decoration, but obedience—to the hand, to the task, to the material.

On Observation
Undated

Men look and do not see. They pass by structure, proportion, and cause as though blind. To observe is to take possession of the world, piece by piece, until nothing is without explanation.
